### 1. How SaaS founders should actually get user validation (and why most advice is wrong)

Published 2026-06-19 · 9 min read · Jeffrin James

Most "validate your SaaS idea" advice tells you to get more signups. That's the worst thing you can do. Signups are not validation — buyers are validation. The fastest way to learn whether anyone will pay for what you've built is to filter your signup flow so only the buyers are left, then go talk to them.

This post is the playbook I'd give a first-time SaaS founder with $5,000 in the bank and three months of runway. It's also the playbook I wish someone had handed me before I built SignupDoggy and watched 38% of my own beta users turn out to be disposable email addresses behind a Tor exit.

What you'll learn:

- Why "get more signups" is the worst advice for early-stage founders.
- The 8-rung validation ladder, from page view to expansion revenue.
- The signup-quality problem: 30–50% of bootstrapped SaaS signup data is bots and throwaways.
- Five things to do this week to actually validate your SaaS idea.
- How to wire SignupDoggy into your signup form in an afternoon.
