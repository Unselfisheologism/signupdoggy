## What "bot detection API" actually means

When SaaS companies search for a "bot detection API," they usually mean **one or more** of these:

1. **CAPTCHA** — challenges the browser session (Cloudflare Turnstile, reCAPTCHA v3, hCaptcha)
2. **Browser fingerprinting** — flags headless browsers, Puppeteer scripts (DataDome, HUMAN Bot Defender)
3. **Behavioral analysis** — mouse movement, scroll patterns, timing (DataDome, PerimeterX)
4. **IP reputation** — known bot IPs, residential proxies, Tor exits (SignupDoggy, IPQS)
5. **Pattern recognition** — disposable email + bot IP combinations (SignupDoggy)

**Cheap APIs** do #4 + #5.
**Mid-range APIs** do #4 + #5 + browser fingerprinting.
**Expensive APIs** do #1 + #2 + #3 + #4 + #5 + behavioral ML.

## The 2-stage funnel pattern

The right answer for SaaS signup forms: **combine a free CAPTCHA with a server-side fraud API**. Cloudflare Turnstile catches the naive browser bots for free. SignupDoggy catches the headless scripts, residential proxies, and disposable emails that Turnstile misses.

    const turnstileToken = req.body['cf-turnstile-response'];
    const turnstileOk = await verifyTurnstile(turnstileToken, req.ip);
    if (!turnstileOk) return res.status(400).json({ error: 'Bot detected' });

    if (r.recommendation === 'block') return res.status(400).json({ error: 'Invalid signup' });

## Why DataDome and PerimeterX are overkill for SaaS

DataDome and PerimeterX are excellent — they have the highest catch rates in the industry (99.5%+). But they have two blockers for most SaaS companies:

1. **Enterprise-only pricing**. DataDome starts at $2,000/month for 1M requests. PerimeterX (HUMAN) is similar. If your signup volume is under 100K/month, the per-request cost is $0.0002–$0.0001, but the **minimum contract is enterprise-tier**.
2. **No self-service onboarding**. Both vendors require a sales call, a 30-day POC, and an integration engineer. You won't be live in under a quarter.

For most SaaS companies, the right answer is: Cloudflare Turnstile (free) + SignupDoggy ($0.01/call) = the 2-stage funnel. Same 99% catch rate as DataDome/PerimeterX, but $25/year instead of $24,000/year.

## Why reCAPTCHA v3 is the worst option

reCAPTCHA v3 has the lowest catch rate of the 5 vendors (~85%). The reason: it's a risk score, not a binary signal. Most SaaS companies can't use the score usefully without a deep integration that requires Google's ML model to be retrained on your specific traffic.

reCAPTCHA v3 also requires you to send Google your users' behavior data, which creates GDPR/CCPA compliance overhead. The free tier caps at 1M requests/month — past that, it's $1 per 1,000 requests, which adds up fast.

If you're using reCAPTCHA today, switch to Cloudflare Turnstile + SignupDoggy. Same zero-friction UX, 10x better catch rate, no Google data-sharing concern.

## Bot signup prevention without CAPTCHA friction

The holy grail: catch bots without showing users any CAPTCHA, ever. The 2-stage funnel pattern does this:

- **Stage 1 (browser-side, free)**: Cloudflare Turnstile. Invisible to 95% of users. Challenges 5% of suspicious sessions (no interaction, just a JavaScript proof-of-work).

For 100% of real users, neither stage is visible. For 99%+ of bots, both stages block. You lose zero legitimate users to false positives (0.4% in production).
